## Why families exist
Project families prevent the portfolio from looking like a pile of unrelated experiments. The public story should show a small number of clear product directions with supporting depth around them.

## Family rules
- Group by buyer, user problem, or product motion.
- Do not group by language or framework alone.
- One repo can map secondarily to another family, but every public project has one primary family.
- Nested workspaces inside a repo are never separate public portfolio entries.

## Family 1: SMB SaaS Operations
Who it serves:
- Small businesses
- Service operators
- Clinics, agencies, local business owners, or recurring-operations teams

What it proves:
- Monetizable SaaS thinking
- Admin workflows
- Business process digitization
- Multi-tenant or role-aware product design

Default members:
- `DevisPilot`
- `UtopiaHire`
- `dentistmanagmentsystem` -> `DentoFlow`
- `trustkit` -> `TrustKit`
- `pricepilot` -> `PricePilot`
- `coffeewebsite` -> `La Storia Di JoJo`

## Presentation guidance
- The homepage should use families as an organizing layer after flagship projects.
- Families should have short narrative intros, not just labels.
- Family cards or filters should help users understand range without overwhelming them.
