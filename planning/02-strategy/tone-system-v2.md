## Objective
Upgrade the brand voice from “clear internal strategy” to “premium external-facing copy” without slipping into hype, startup clichés, or fake authority.

## Tone traits
- vivid
- earned
- precise
- calm
- premium
- high-agency
- product-minded
- operational

## Tone should not be
- loud
- founder-post style
- inflated
- generic student-portfolio language
- empty motivational language
- abstract management-speak

## Preferred language patterns
### 1. Contrast framing
Use:
- Not just features. Systems that can actually run.
- Not just prototypes. Products with operational weight.

Purpose:
- sharpens the positioning quickly
- creates memorability without exaggeration

### 2. Operational verbs over abstract nouns
Prefer:
- shape
- de-risk
- structure
- tighten
- orchestrate
- harden
- align
- move
- turn
- simplify

Avoid overusing:
- execution
- supervision
- ambition
- readiness
- thinking

### 3. Consequence-first phrasing
Prefer:
- I help rough ideas become clearer, tighter, and more launchable.
- I turn product chaos into systems, milestones, and usable software.

Over:
- I focus on structured software delivery.

### 4. Mess-to-momentum framing
Use when defensible:
- from messy ideas to working product systems
- from scattered product energy to coherent delivery
- from raw concept to something a team can build and a market can understand

### 5. Serious-product language
Prefer:
- serious product systems
- operationally useful software
- products with workflow weight
- software built to be used, not just shown
- systems that survive contact with the real world

Over:
- ambitious bets
- world-class
- disruptive

## Forbidden or discouraged language
- visionary
- future of everything
- 10x
- world-class
- disrupting
- founder-minded unless directly relevant
- building the future
- end-to-end repeated as filler
- ambitious bets as a repeated slogan
- passionate developer as a lead phrase

## Hero/headline direction ladder
### Safest
- Product-minded engineering for teams that need more than code.
- Building serious software across product, systems, and delivery.

### Balanced
- From messy ideas to working product systems.
- I turn product chaos into clear systems, delivery structure, and shipped software.

### Boldest safe range
- I build software that can survive contact with the real world.
- Not just shipping features. Shaping products that can actually run.

## About-section direction
- Lead with consequence, not biography.
- Mention long-horizon coding history early.
- Show that university is part of the story, not the start of the story.
- Tie the “builder + director” role to product systems, not vague leadership labels.

## Copy rules by surface
### Website
- most vivid
- shortest
- strongest use of contrast and system language

### CV
- most compressed
- strongest proof discipline
- less rhetorical flourish

### GitHub
- technical trust
- practical and direct
- product framing supported by repo reality

### LinkedIn
- recruiter-readable
- ambitious but composed
- one degree less sharp than the homepage hero

## Proof guardrail
If a line cannot be backed by a project story in two sentences, it should not be a hero-level claim.

## Reusable phrases
- long-horizon builder
- builder + director
- software with operational weight
- product systems that can actually run
- clearer, tighter, more launchable
- systems people can depend on
- disciplined product execution
