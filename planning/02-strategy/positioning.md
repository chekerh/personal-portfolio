## Core positioning statement
Cheker Hassan is a long-horizon builder, product engineer, and builder + director who turns ambitious software ideas into structured delivery: clarifying product direction, framing the roadmap, supervising execution, and pushing systems toward launch across web, backend, AI, developer tooling, and operational software.

## Market narrative
This portfolio should not read as “a student with several coding projects.” It should read as:
- a builder with years of technical depth before university and before the AI hype cycle
- a product-minded engineer with strong execution instincts
- a builder who can move from concept to delivery structure
- someone comfortable supervising multi-part software bets and active product teams
- a candidate suited for Greece-based and remote-first teams that value ownership, speed, and systems thinking

## Positioning pillars
### 1. Operator-builder
Core message:
- ideas do not stay abstract; they become plans, workflows, milestones, and shipped software

Proof signals:
- Jira planning and sprint framing
- delivery supervision across multiple products
- GitHub workflows and structured execution
- launch-oriented thinking instead of isolated coding
- long-term technical consistency across multiple eras of tooling

### 2. Product engineer
Core message:
- engineering work is tied to user value, business logic, and product maturity

Proof signals:
- products across SaaS, trust systems, tooling, AI workflows, and operations
- attention to admin flows, operations, and usability
- systems that go beyond tutorial-style CRUD framing

### 3. Delivery supervision
Core message:
- value comes not only from writing code, but from helping software get organized, coordinated, and completed

Proof signals:
- roadmap thinking
- technical direction across several apps
- QA, implementation structure, and launch readiness
- coordination between frontend, backend, data, and product requirements

### 4. Ambitious software bets
Core message:
- the work aims higher than small demos and shows a preference for meaningful systems with startup, operational, or social-impact potential

Proof signals:
- local-first and AI-assisted workflows
- multi-surface products with dashboards, mobile, and backend components
- business operations and automation systems
- developer tools with clear technical ambition
