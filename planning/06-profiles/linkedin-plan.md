## Objective
Make LinkedIn the recruiter-facing version of the operator-builder story: clear headline, strong About section, credible project framing, and explicit alignment with Greece-based and remote-first opportunities.

## Positioning goal
LinkedIn should present Cheker Hassan as:
- a product engineer
- an operator-builder
- someone with delivery supervision instincts
- a candidate for ambitious software teams in Greece and remote markets

## Headline direction
Recommended version:
**Product Engineer | Operator-Builder | SaaS, AI, Tooling, and Delivery Systems | Open to Greece and Remote Roles**

Alternative versions:
1. **Operator-Builder and Product Engineer | Structured Software Delivery Across SaaS, AI, and Developer Tools**
2. **Product-Minded Software Engineer | Delivery Supervision, Full-Stack Builds, and Ambitious Software Bets**
3. **Software Engineer and Operator-Builder | Building Product Systems for Greece and Remote Teams**

## About section plan
The About section should have three parts.

### Part 1. Identity
Open with a strong first sentence:
- I’m a product-minded software engineer and operator-builder focused on turning ambitious ideas into structured software execution.

### Part 2. What I work on
Include 4 to 6 lines covering:
- SaaS, trust systems, developer tooling, and AI-enabled products
- roadmap and delivery structure
- technical supervision across multi-part systems
- business tooling and practical operations software
- preference for launch-oriented work over isolated experiments

### Part 3. What I’m looking for
Close with explicit market direction:
- I’m especially interested in opportunities in Greece and remote-first teams where engineering, product ownership, and execution discipline matter together.

## Experience framing
Experience entries should not undersell the work as scattered personal projects. Use role framing that reflects execution and supervision honestly.

Suggested framing themes:
- Product and Delivery Supervision
- Multi-Project Software Builder
- AI and Operational Systems Development
- Trust and Workflow Systems Engineering

Each entry should include:
- what kind of systems were built
- what execution responsibilities were handled
- what coordination or structure was involved
- what technologies or domains were central

## Content strategy
LinkedIn posts should reinforce the brand, not act as generic motivation content.

Recommended categories:
### 1. Build-in-public updates
- product progress
- architecture milestones
- feature launches
- lessons from active builds

### 2. Operator notes
- how to structure a roadmap
- what changes when thinking like a product engineer
- how delivery supervision improves outcomes
- what makes a software project launchable

### 3. Project breakdowns
- CheckAuto TN as a trust workflow system
- DevisPilot as SMB SaaS
- Sahara as a developer tool
- graphify as technical/product differentiation

### 4. Market alignment
- what kind of roles you are targeting
- why Greece is a strong direction
- how you work in remote-first environments
- what you want to build next

## Tone rules
LinkedIn should sound:
- ambitious
- structured
- credible
- product-minded
- internationally employable

Avoid:
- vague inspiration posts
- exaggerated founder language
- generic student framing
- long lists of tools with no context

## Success criteria
LinkedIn is working if:
- the headline immediately explains the profile
- the About section matches the portfolio narrative
- recruiters can see both technical ability and delivery ownership
- the profile clearly supports Greece-based and remote-first opportunities
