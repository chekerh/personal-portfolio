## Section order
1. Hero
2. Positioning / About

4. Directed Products
5. Skills / Systems
6. Proof Strip
7. Contact / CTA

## Section-by-section implementation targets
### 1. Hero
- desktop target:
  - eyebrow
  - 2 to 3 line headline
  - 1 compact role badge
  - 1 support paragraph
  - 2 CTA buttons
- mobile target:
  - same structure
  - shorter support line
  - stacked CTAs
- must not include:
  - a full sentence inside the giant animated headline
  - more than one long paragraph
  - more than four visual chips

## Hero purpose
- establish long-horizon builder identity
- avoid student-first framing
- introduce builder + director energy
- signal product seriousness fast

## Hero copy direction
- headline: short, vivid, premium
- subheadline: clarify builder + director role
- support line: mention 11 years coding, product systems, and active product direction

### Hero content constraints
- `headline`:
  - 6 to 14 words
  - should feel premium, not slogan-heavy
- `role badge`:
  - 2 to 5 words
  - example direction: `Builder + director`
- `support line`:
  - 18 to 34 words
  - must include at least one proof signal
- `primary CTA`:
  - `View resume` or `Explore work`
- `secondary CTA`:
  - `LinkedIn` or `GitHub`

## About purpose
- explain the working model:
  - ideas
  - product structure
  - supervision
  - technical contribution
- mention that multiple teams/products are under direction

### 2. Positioning / About
- content target:
  - 1 section heading
  - 1 core positioning paragraph
  - 4 positioning cards max
  - 1 compact timeline or experience strip
- must answer:
  - what kind of builder is Cheker
  - how he works
  - what kinds of systems he builds
  - what teams should hire him for

## Flagship section purpose
- show the most publicly defensible products
- emphasize proof, product quality, and technical seriousness

### 3. Flagship Projects
- homepage target:
  - 3 flagship blocks in large format
  - optional 4th flagship only if layout remains clean
- each flagship block must contain:
  - title
  - one-line tagline
  - 40 to 70 word summary
  - stack line
  - role line
  - proof-level label
  - primary CTA
  - secondary CTA if available
- default flagship homepage candidates:
  - `CheckAuto TN`
  - `DevisPilot`
  - `Sahara`
- `graphify` belongs in top support unless its ownership/public framing becomes cleaner

### 4. Directed Products
- homepage target:
  - 4 to 6 cards
  - compact but substantial
- each card must contain:
  - title
  - status label: `In development`
  - 25 to 45 word summary
  - role framing
  - safe CTA
- default homepage candidates:
  - `Local-Arcads`
  - `FlashForge`
  - `Context Assistant`
  - `SignLik`
  - `CogniCare`
- `WayFinder` and `YouClipper` can stay catalog-only unless visual proof improves

## Skills section purpose
- show capability groups tied to real work
- include delivery/system skills alongside technical stack

### 5. Skills / Systems
- do not use a flat logo wall
- use 4 groups:
  - product systems
  - frontend and app surfaces
  - backend and data
  - delivery and ops
- each group must have:
  - title
  - 1 short descriptor
  - 4 to 8 items
- every group should map back to projects

## Proof strip purpose
- compress trust signals into a scannable band
- candidates:
  - 11 years coding
  - multi-product delivery structure
  - local-first AI + SaaS + tooling range
  - multilingual communication

### 6. Proof Strip
- homepage target:
  - 4 proof items
- default items:
  - `11 years` / coding depth
  - `multi-product` / supervised delivery
  - `SaaS + AI + tooling` / systems range
  - `Arabic / French / English` / communication range
- avoid vanity metrics without evidence

## Contact / CTA purpose
- make outreach easy
- present portfolio, GitHub, LinkedIn, and CV as a coherent system

### 7. Contact / CTA
- target:
  - 1 concise paragraph
  - 3 links max
- preferred links:
  - email
  - LinkedIn
  - CV
- GitHub can appear elsewhere and does not need to dominate the close

## Project surface rules
- homepage shows:
  - 3 flagships in large format
  - 4 to 6 directed products in compact format
  - top support can appear in a secondary catalog row
- homepage does not need to show every project in equal visual weight
- every visible project must map to the proof bank or directed-products registry

## Copy approval rules
- homepage may sound sharper than the CV
- no section should rely on empty superlatives
- every strong line should connect to one of:
  - years of coding
  - product structuring
  - supervised delivery
  - serious product architecture
- if a line cannot be defended in a walkthrough, it should not ship

## Boldness rule
- homepage can be sharper than CV/LinkedIn
- no fake grandeur
- strong contrast language is allowed if backed by project reality
